    DBM150 Final: Balloon car



    This Final DBM150 project made use of both SolidWorks and the laser cutter that is available to use on campus. The body and axl of the car were made in SolidWorks to be printed with PLA. The body is small and hollow to to be as light as possible, and the axls are printed in 4 parts to make sure the wheels stay attached. Speaking of the wheels, these were not 3D printed, rather, they were sketched in Solidworks before being sent to the campus' laser cutter to create flat wooden wheels. The final part to this project is the balloon and straws that are glued to the top of the car. This project shows a innovative proof of concept for air-powered transport.

    DBM215 Mini Project 2: Warhammer Puzzle Box




    The Warhammer puzzle box was the second mini project I did for DBM215. It is a storage box for Warhammer miniatures. Its main feature is the magnetic plates that you place the minifigures on. These plates can be taken out of the box and be arranged in any order that the user desires. The box was modeled through SolidWorks in 3 parts, being the top, the bottom, and the Axl that allows the box to open and close. On the inside of the box, I glued a metal sheet, so the magnetic display plates stuck to the box. The display plates themselves were also modeled in SolidWorks, and after they were printed, I glued magnets on their backs. I incorporated magnetic surfaces into this build begause I wanted to prevent the plates from falling out of the box in the event that it gets tipped over turned upside down. The velcrow straps glued to the front also help keep the plates inside the box because they allow the box to stay closed.

    4. SIP Project: The Hybrid Controller

    The Hybrid Controller is my SIP project that combines the analog controls of an Xbox controller with the button layout of an arcade stick. The shell of the controller (This includes the left analog stick and the button box on the right) and the button caps were made in SolidWorks and then printed with PLA material. For both sides of the controller, I had to print a top and bottom and the parts and then fasten then together with screws. The button caps were designed to slot into mechanical keyboard switches due the the inserts I put on the bottom. The left side has a greater balance between form and function than the right since it is made to be ergonomic while also having the necessary input devieces to play games. The right side is soley based on function, as the rectangular shape and sharp edges lead to a very non-ergonomic shape.

    6. RBT173 Final: Wireless RC car



    This was my final project for RBT173 in which I chose to make a wireless RC car. The car itself is made from a plastic base with a breadboard on top. The breadboard connects an Arduino to two motors (they function as the back wheels) and a nRF24l01 component, which itself wirelessly connects to another Arduino that is wired up to be the car’s controller.
