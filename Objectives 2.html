    DBM150 Final: Balloon car





    My Final DBM150 project made use of both SolidWorks and the laser cutter that was available to use on campus. The body and axels of the car were made in SolidWorks to be printed with PLA. The former was made small and hollow to be as light as possible to allow the car to move from the air coming out of a balloon, while the latter were printed in 4 parts to make sure the wheels stay attached. Speaking of the wheels, they were sketched in SolidWorks before being sent to the campus' laser cutter to create them out of flat wooden planes. The wheels, axels, and body were assembled using adhesives, mainly superglue. The final part of this project is the balloon and straws that are glued to the top of the car. This project shows an innovative proof of concept for air-powered transport.

    DBM215 Mini Project 2: Warhammer Puzzle Box





    The Warhammer puzzle box was the second mini project I did for DBM215. It is a storage box for Warhammer minifigures that contains magnetic plates to place them on. They plates come in various shapes and sizes to fit different sizes of minifigures and can be arranged in any order that can fit in the box. The box and magnetic plates were designed in SolidWorks and printed with PLA material. The display plates have magnet sheets glued to the back which stick to a thin sheet of galvanized steel on the bottom of the box. On the front of the box are Velcro straps that can be used to keep the box closed, and combined with the magnets inside, helps to prevent the plates and minifigures from falling out of the box.

    4. SIP Project: The Hybrid Controller



    The Hybrid Controller is my SIP project that combines the analog controls of an Xbox controller with the button layout of an arcade stick. The shell of the controller (This includes the left analog stick and the button box on the right) and the button caps were designed in SolidWorks and then printed with PLA material. For both sides of the controller, I had to print a top and bottom and the parts and then fasten then together with screws. The button caps were designed to slot into mechanical keyboard switches due the the inserts I put on the bottom. The left side has a greater balance between form and function than the right since it is made to be ergonomic while also having the necessary input devieces to play games. The right side is soley based on function, as the rectangular shape and sharp edges lead to a very non-ergonomic shape. I believe this can become a future trend, as I have been going to local fighting game events with The Hybrid Controller and people have been complementing me for my innovative design.

    6. RBT173 Final: Wireless RC car





    This was my final project for RBT173 in which I chose to make a wireless RC car. The car itself is made from a plastic base with a breadboard on top. The breadboard connects an Arduino to two motors (they function as the back wheels) and a nRF24l01 component, which itself wirelessly connects to another Arduino that is wired up to be the car’s controller.
